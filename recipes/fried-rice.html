Fried Rice

Prep Time: 20 minutes

Cook Time: 7 hours 15 minutes

Additional Time: 1 hour

Total Time: 8 hours 25 minutes

Servings: 4 people

Description

Got some leftover rice? Fry it! Commonly appearing in eastern cuisines, fried rice is one of my favorite comfort foods thanks to its rich taste and aroma. Plus I just love rice.

One of the best things about it is how versatile the recipe is, allowing for all sorts of ingredients if you dare to put your mind to it. Whether as part of a course, or a whole meal by itself, it's bound to fill you up and leave you satisfied!

Ingredients

  • 2/3 cup chopped baby carrots
  • 1/2 cup frozen green peas
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 clove garlic, minced or to taste
  • 2 large eggs
  • 3 cups leftover cooked and chilled white rice
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce or more to taste
  • 2 teaspoons sesame oil or to taste

Steps

  1. Place carrots in a small saucepan and cover with water. Bring to a low boil, then cook for 3 to 5 minutes. Stir the peas in, then immediately drain in a colander.
  2. Put a wok over high heat. Pour in vegetable oil for wok hey, then stir in carrots, peas and garlic. Cook for approx. 30 seconds, then add eggs. Stir them quickly to scramble them with the vegetables.
  3. Stir in the cooked rice. Add soy sauce and toss rice to coat, then drizzle with sesame oil and toss again.
  4. Serve and enjoy!
